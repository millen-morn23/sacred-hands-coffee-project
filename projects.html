Farmer Voices

A storytelling initiative showcasing the lives and challenges of smallholder coffee farmers through photos and narratives. We believe that every farmer has a story worth telling, and these stories help bridge the gap between coffee consumers and the people who grow their daily cup.

Through intimate portraits and personal interviews, we document the dedication, struggles, and triumphs of our farming partners, creating a human connection that goes beyond the transactional nature of commodity coffee.

Hands for Msitu

Our reforestation project planting indigenous trees in coffee communities to combat climate change and preserve biodiversity

Why Indigenous Trees Matter

Climate change poses significant challenges to coffee farming in Western Kenya. By planting indigenous trees, we're helping farmers adapt to changing weather patterns while preserving local ecosystems and traditional knowledge.

Indigenous trees provide shade for coffee plants, prevent soil erosion, improve water retention, and create habitats for beneficial wildlife that supports coffee production.
